In philosophy, "The Other" simply refers to **another human being** who is not you.
It sounds simple, right? But Levinas argues that *how* we think about this other person determines whether we treat them ethically or just use them.

* **The Other as an Object:** They believed that when you meet someone else, you are basically studying them like an object to be understood.
* **The Mirror Effect:** They thought, *"Oh, that person is just like me! They are an 'alter-ego'."*

**The Problem:**
