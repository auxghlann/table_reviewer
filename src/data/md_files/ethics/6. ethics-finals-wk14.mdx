## ðŸ§© Chunk 3: Cyber-bullying

### ðŸ“µ Defining Cyber-bullying
Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices (phones, computers) via SMS, apps, or social media.

**Behaviors include:**
- Sending/posting harmful, false, or mean content.
- Sharing private information to cause humiliation.
- Creating a permanent public record that damages **Online Reputation**.

### ðŸ›ï¸ Institutional Policies (USL Student Handbook)
Universities often classify cyber-bullying as a **Major Offense**.

| Offense Type | Description | Sanction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Misuse of Facilities** | Posting obscene, injurious, or harassing messages/photos. | Suspension to Non-readmission (1st Offense). |
| **Bullying Types** | Verbal, text, physical, cyber, serial, or gang bullying. | Non-readmission to Exclusion/Dismissal (2nd Offense). |

### ðŸ§­ Ethical Perspective
Cyber-bullying is not just a rule violation; it is a **moral failure**.
1.  **Violation of Dignity:** We are created in God's image; respecting oneself and neighbors is a humane obligation.
2.  **Injustice:** It harms the innocent and disrupts community peace.
3.  **Root Sin:** It is often incited by **Pride**.

> **The Lesson:** As a student, your ethical obligation is to respect others and avoid offenses that damage the dignity of your neighbor.
